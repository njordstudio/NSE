# NJORD Engine , Public Roadmap

What we are building and what is planned. This is a **living, unofficial** list ,
priorities shift, and a lot of it grows from **your** bug reports and
[feature requests](../../issues/new/choose). No dates or promises; it shows
direction.

> Have an idea or a "it should also do X"? Open a **Feature request** issue.
> Hit a bug? Open a **Bug report**. Both feed straight into this roadmap.

## Now (early access / bug-test builds)

- Double-click-to-play Windows builds for testing.
- Core genres playable: **platformer**, **shooter**, **racing**.
- Characters with animations, enemies (chase / patrol / ranged), collectibles,
  hazards, goals, checkpoints, win/lose rules.
- Shooter: aim camera, weapons + ammo/reload, per-bone hitboxes (headshots),
  shootable buttons/switches, explosive barrels (area damage).
- Racing: drivable vehicle, drift + handbrake, tracks + laps, AI opponents.
- A HUD, dialogue/NPCs/quests, sound, save/load, an in-game options menu
  (volume, resolution, key rebinding).

## Next

- More polish across the three genres from your feedback.
- Richer effects (particles/smoke), more audio.
- A visual in-editor tuning pass (hitboxes, colliders, tracks).
- Smoother animation transitions and blending.

## Planned

- **More platforms:** native Linux and macOS builds (Windows-only today).
- **Distribution:** Steam release.
- **RPG foundations:** stats/leveling, inventory/items, shops.
- **Deeper worlds:** multi-level games, larger scenes.
- Quality-of-life across the editor and the shipped games.

## Ideas / under consideration

- Gamepad support.
- Online/multiplayer (the engine's simulation is already built to be
  netcode-friendly).
- An in-browser editor (the engine already builds to the web).
- Local / offline AI (point the engine at a model on your own machine).
- Community-driven templates and starter games.
